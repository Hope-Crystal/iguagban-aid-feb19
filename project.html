Our Projects

Check out our work and case studies.

School Building

How We Did It

Due to the dilapidated and fast fading school building in our Local Community, our children have been unable to lear and get quality education. The community in diaspora have been able to demolish the old structure and in the process of erecting a new structure which will be able to accommodate children from nearby communities. Although this project is not yet finished we are excited about our progress and encourage others to join in sponsoring this project.

Road Construction

How We Did It

The Iguagban Community is an indigenous society which is made of predominantly farmers who specialises in farming different agricultural products. The poor state of the road have hindered the transportation of these produce to development. Work has been done through the NGO to increase the sizes of existing road networks and big machineries deployed to grade the road to aid easy access of motorist. A lot of work and resources have been done to this present state. There is still a lot that need to be done

Borehole

How We Did It

As recorded in WHO gazette, the foremost cause of death of young children in Africa is cholera and other water related illnesses. As a United Community Organization, we have been able to provide clean water at various designated areas around the community and also alternative energy to ensure the constant supply of pipe-borne water. This is a project that spans across various communities. The pictures below show some of this locations where this projects are cited. There is still a long way to go to ensure that every community has at least 3 sources of cleaning water.
